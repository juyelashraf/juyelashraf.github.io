About Me

Welcome to my website! I am Juyel Ashraf, a master’s student and Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Agribusiness and Applied Economics at North Dakota State University, Fargo, ND.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Energy Economics, Labor Economics, and International Economics. My current work spans topics including trade openness and CO₂ emissions, female labor force participation in developing economies, nitrogen application under risk, and the economic feasibility of livestock management practices.

I work as a Graduate Research Assistant under Dr. Jon T. Biermacher and have previously worked under Dr. Robert Hearne. I also serve as the Vice President for Public Relations of the Agribusiness & Applied Economics Graduate Club at NDSU.
