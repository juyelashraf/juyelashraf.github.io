My Universities

A reflection on the two universities that have shaped my academic journey — from the hills of Chittagong to the plains of North Dakota.

University of Chittagong Undergraduate · Alma Mater

Chittagong, Bangladesh  |  Established 1966

The University of Chittagong (Bengali: চট্টগ্রাম বিশ্ববিদ্যালয়) is a public research university located in Hathazari Upazila, Chittagong District, Bangladesh — about 22 kilometres north of Chittagong city. Founded on 18 November 1966, it is one of the largest universities in Bangladesh, spanning a sprawling campus of 2,312 acres (9.36 km²) nestled among the Sitakund Hills and lush forests. The campus is served by its own scenic shuttle train, which winds through the greenery to connect the university with the city — one of the most distinctive features of student life there.

As of 2024, the university offers programmes across 54 departments under 9 faculties, catering to thousands of undergraduate and postgraduate students each year. The Chittagong University Central Library is the largest library in Chittagong and one of the richest in the country, housing approximately 350,000 items including rare manuscripts, journals, and Braille books.

Location: Fatehpur, Hathazari Upazila, Chittagong, Bangladesh

Type: Public Research University

Founded: 18 November 1966

Campus Area: 2,312 acres

Faculties: 9  |  Departments: 54

Website: cu.ac.bd

Department of Economics

I completed my undergraduate studies in the Department of Economics, one of the founding departments of the university. The department began offering MA (Preliminary) classes under the Faculty of Arts on 18 November 1966 — the very day the university was inaugurated — with 200 students and seven faculty members. Since then it has grown into a distinguished centre of economic education in Bangladesh, offering BSS (Honours), MSS (Preliminary), and MSS (Final) programmes. The department publishes Eco, a peer-reviewed research journal, and has produced some of Bangladesh's most eminent economists and policymakers.

The department's website: econ-cu.netlify.app  |  Facebook page

Notable Professor: Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Dr. Muhammad Yunus — Nobel Peace Prize Laureate (2006)

Perhaps the most celebrated scholar to have taught at the Department of Economics, Dr. Muhammad Yunus joined the department as an assistant professor in 1972 and later served as its head. Witnessing the devastating famine of 1974, he was moved to extend his work beyond the classroom — an impulse that ultimately gave birth to the modern concept of microcredit.

In 1983, he established Grameen Bank, providing small loans on easy terms to the rural poor, transforming millions of lives across Bangladesh and inspiring microfinance institutions worldwide. Dr. Yunus and Grameen Bank were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 — making him the first Bangladeshi to receive this honour.

North Dakota State University (NDSU) Graduate

Fargo, North Dakota, USA  |  Established 1890

North Dakota State University (NDSU) is a public land-grant research university in Fargo, North Dakota, United States. Originally founded as North Dakota Agricultural College in 1890, it was the state's first land-grant institution. The first class of students was admitted on September 8, 1891, and the institution was formally renamed North Dakota State University on November 8, 1960, following a statewide referendum — reflecting its expanding breadth of academic programmes.

Today, NDSU is classified among R1 Doctoral Universities — designating “very high research activity” — and is a proud member of the North Dakota University System. As of 2021, the university offers 94 undergraduate majors, 87 master's degree programmes, and 51 doctoral degree programmes.

Location: Fargo, North Dakota 58102, USA

Type: Public Land-Grant Research University (R1)

Founded: 1890

Programmes: 94 undergraduate majors · 87 master's · 51 doctoral

Website: ndsu.edu

Department of Agribusiness and Applied Economics

I am currently enrolled as an MS student and serve as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Agribusiness and Applied Economics (AAEC). The department has a distinguished history — having served the people of North Dakota since its formation in 1918 — and is housed in Richard H. Barry Hall on the NDSU Fargo campus.

The department's mission is to provide objective economic analysis supporting informed decision-making in areas of resource allocation, business management, and public policy for the people of North Dakota and beyond. Graduate programmes include:

  • MS in Agribusiness and Applied Economics — combining economic theory, agribusiness, and quantitative research methods.
  • MS in International Agribusiness — with language training and international work experience.
  • Agribusiness MBA — equipping managers of agribusiness firms with specialised decision-making skills.

It is this rich research environment that drew me to NDSU, where I currently work under Dr. Jon T. Biermacher on topics including nitrogen application under risk and the economic feasibility of livestock management practices.

Department website: ndsu.edu/agriculture/agribusiness-applied-economics
